Research

Deep Learning for Multi-Omic Integration

I am developing interpretable methods for multi-omic data integration to link diverse biological layers such as gene expression, epigenomics, and proteomics. This work aims to clarify how molecular systems jointly influence health and disease.

Status Ongoing project

Collaborators: William Stafford Noble , MOHD Consortium

Protein Language Models and Protein Science

I develop and analyze protein language models (PLMs) to understand how evolutionary information is encoded in protein sequences. As an early contributor who helped pioneer the field, my work spans foundational transformer models, specialized models for intrinsically disordered proteins, and recent studies showing that PLMs capture epistasis, fitness landscapes, and evolutionary constraints.

Selected publications:

  • Protein Language Models Capture Structural and Functional Epistasis in a Zero-Shot Setting (bioRxiv, 2025)
  • DR-BERT: A Protein Language Model to Annotate Disordered Regions (Structure, 2024)
  • Transforming the Language of Life: Transformer Neural Networks for Protein Prediction Tasks (ACM BCB, 2020)
  • Machine Learning for Protein Science and Engineering (Cold Spring Harbor Perspectives in Biology, 2025)

Collaborators: Sergei Maslov, Mark Hopkins, Anna Ritz, Shivani Ahuja , Greg Anderson

Machine-Learning-Guided GWAS and Disease Prediction

I apply machine learning to enhance genome-wide association studies, integrating clinical, environmental, and genetic data to uncover patterns underlying complex diseases.

Status Ongoing project

Collaborators: Sergei Maslov, Sharon Donovan, Konstantinos Lazaridis (Mayo), Alina Allen

Information Theory for Diagnostics

I applied ideas from information theory to pathogen diagnosis. Our semi-quantitative group testing framework enables efficient, accurate two-stage qPCR screening across wide load ranges while preserving quantitative resolution.

Selected publication:

  • Semi-Quantitative Group Testing for Efficient and Accurate qPCR Screening of Pathogens with a Wide Range of Loads (BMC Bioinformatics, 2024)

Collaborators: Sergei Maslov (UIUC), Olgica Milenkovic (UIUC).

Evolution and Classification of Technology

I investigate innovation dynamics, diversity, and recombination in technological ecosystems using patent and product datasets. We modeled how novelty sustains open-ended innovation and quantified a long-term decline in product diversity among large U.S. firms.

Selected publications:

  • Open-Ended Technological Innovation (Artificial Life, 2019)
  • Dropping Diversity of Products of Large US Firms: Models and Measures (PLOS ONE, 2022)

Collaborators: Mark Bedau (Reed College), Norman Packard (ProtoLife), Tobias Rubel (UMD), J. Doyne Farmer (Oxford).

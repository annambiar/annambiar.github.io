Research

My research focuses on developing interpretable machine learning models for complex biological systems— from multi-omic data integration and protein language models to GWAS-driven disease discovery and the study of evolutionary processes in both biology and technology.

Multi-Omic Integration and Imputation

I am developing interpretable methods for multi-omic data integration to link diverse biological layers such as gene expression, epigenomics, and proteomics. This work aims to improve our understanding of how molecular systems jointly influence health and disease.

Status: Ongoing project

Collaborators: William Stafford Noble (University of Washington) and colleagues in the MOHD Consortium.

Protein Language Models and Protein Science

I study how protein language models (PLMs) capture structure, function, and evolution from sequence alone. My work demonstrates that PLMs encode residue dependencies, predict mutation effects, and represent higher-order epistasis, with strong zero-shot generalization to experimental fitness landscapes.

Selected publications:
• Protein Language Models Capture Structural and Functional Epistasis in a Zero-Shot Setting (bioRxiv, 2025)
• DR-BERT: A Protein Language Model to Annotate Disordered Regions (Structure, 2024)
• Transforming the Language of Life: Transformer Neural Networks for Protein Prediction Tasks (ACM BCB, 2020)
• Machine Learning for Protein Science and Engineering (CSH Perspectives in Biology, 2025)

Collaborators: Mark Hopkins (Reed College), Anna Ritz (Reed College), Sergei Maslov (UIUC), Simon Liu (NYU), Patrick K. Koo.

Machine-Learning-Guided GWAS and Disease Prediction

I apply machine learning to enhance genome-wide association studies, integrating clinical, environmental, and genetic data to uncover patterns that underlie complex diseases.

Status: Ongoing project

Collaborators: William Stafford Noble (University of Washington) and the Mayo Clinic Center for Individualized Medicine.

Transcriptional Regulation and Functional Genomics

I build models linking promoter sequence, transcription-factor activity, and chromatin context to gene-expression programs. In FUN-PROSE, we predicted condition-specific expression in fungi from sequence and TF abundance, revealing interpretable motif–TF relationships. I also contributed to a transcriptomic atlas of acid-stress response in multiple Issatchenkia orientalis strains, mapping conserved regulatory pathways underlying adaptation.

Selected publications:
• FUN-PROSE: A Deep Learning Approach to Predict Condition-Specific Gene Expression in Fungi (PLoS Comput Biol, 2023)
• A Transcriptomic Atlas of Acute Stress Response to Low pH in Multiple Issatchenkia orientalis Strains (Microbiology Spectrum, 2024)

Collaborators: Veronika Dubinkina (Gladstone Institutes), Sergei Maslov (UIUC), Simon Liu (NYU).

Diagnostics and Complex-Systems Methodology

I apply ideas from information theory and complex systems to biomedical diagnostics. Our semi-quantitative group testing framework enables efficient, accurate two-stage qPCR screening of pathogens across wide load ranges, achieving high throughput while preserving quantitative resolution.

Selected publication: Semi-Quantitative Group Testing for Efficient and Accurate qPCR Screening of Pathogens with a Wide Range of Loads (BMC Bioinformatics, 2024)

Collaborators: Mojtaba Cheraghchi (Imperial College London), Sergei Maslov (UIUC).

Evolution and Classification of Technology

I investigate innovation dynamics, diversity, and recombination in technological ecosystems using patent and product datasets. We modeled how novelty sustains open-ended innovation and quantified a long-term decline in product diversity among large U.S. firms.

Selected publications:
• Open-Ended Technological Innovation (Artificial Life, 2019)
• Dropping Diversity of Products of Large US Firms: Models and Measures (PLOS ONE, 2022)

Collaborators: Mark Bedau (Reed College), Norman Packard (ProtoLife), Tobias Rubel (UMD), J. Doyne Farmer (Oxford).
